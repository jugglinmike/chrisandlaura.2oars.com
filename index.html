Our Story

"Tu de este lado y yo del otro como dos remos," Trans. "You on one side and I on the other, like two oars."

This verse is featured in Love Park in Lima, Peru, which was conceived by Peruvian poet Antonio Cillóniz. Chris and Laura visited the park in 2009 after graduating from Harvard together, and they continue to be amazed at how appropriately the quote they discovered there applies to their relationship. Both active in the intramural sports community in college, Chris and Laura became friends while co-captaining the Lowell House crew team--their heads together planning boat lineups, early morning workouts on the Charles River, and big team breakfasts characterized their first interactions together. It was one such early morning in 2008 when Chris asked Laura to Lowell's spring formal, the Bacchanalia.

The happy couple has been together ever since, their relationship growing and flourishing with equal support and love from port and starboard sides. Together for three years in Cambridge and Boston, they parted ways to pursue higher education in Atlanta and Philadelphia-- Chris on one side of the Mason Dixon Line and Laura on the other-- and after two years they emerged even stronger and more resolute in their love. They are so excited to begin their Happily Ever After-- together again, at last-- in the city of Atlanta, Georgia, where they will remain until Chris completes his MD/PhD at Emory University.
